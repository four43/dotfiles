Extracted from brand identity screenshots. Source: Vaisala Xweather Brand Identity guide.

## 1. Cover — Xweather Identity (p.1)

Title page: "Xweather identity" in large light teal/mint text on a dark background (Xweather Black). Vaisala Xweather logo in bottom-left corner. Sets the overall tone: dark, minimal, modern.

---

## 2. Logo Color Versions (p.8, Section 2.0 Logo)

Two primary logo color versions: **dark** and **light**.

- **Dark logo** (black): used on lighter backgrounds
- **White logo**: used on darker backgrounds (Xweather Black)

Color accessibility must be checked before use. In special cases the logo may use other main brand colors, but requires marketing team approval.

---

## 3. Logo Clear Space (p.9, Section 2.0 Logo)

The clear space around the logo should equal its **optical height (x)**, not actual height, since the logo is vertically unbalanced.

- Descenders and extending elements should NOT be used to determine margins/clear space
- Clear space is measured from the optical height of the main letterforms
- Diagram shows x-height spacing on all four sides

---

## 4. Logo Alignment (p.10, Section 2.0 Logo)

- Logo should be **anchored to one corner** of the layout (choose from four corners depending on context)
- Alternative: **center the logo** within the layout
- Diagrams show the four-corner placement options and centered option with alignment guides

---

## 5. Symbol (p.12, Section 2.1 Symbol)

The Xweather **"X" symbol** is derived from the logo. It's a stylized X mark with angular, dynamic strokes.

- Used exclusively in specific digital applications
- **Should never be combined with the logo**
- Requires prior approval from the marketing team

---

## 6. Symbol Color Versions (p.13, Section 2.1 Symbol)

Three color combinations for the symbol in its container (rounded square):

1. **Misty Green Extra Light** symbol on **Xweather Black** container
2. **Xweather Black** symbol in **Misty Green Extra Light** container
3. **White** symbol in **Xweather Black** container

---

## 7. Symbol Clear Space (p.14, Section 2.1 Symbol)

- Symbol appears within a **square or circular container** in digital applications
- Clear space is equal to the width of the symbol's highest or lowest part
- In circular containers, clear space is measured based on a rectangle the width of the symbol that fits within the circle
- Symbol should always be **horizontally centered**

## 10. Typography in Use (p.19, Section 3.0 Typography)

Apply the **Golden Ratio** for font sizes (heading = ~1.618x body text).

| Element | Size | Weight |
|---------|------|--------|
| H1 | 144pt | Light |
| H2 | 89pt | Light |
| H3 | 55pt | Light |
| Ingress/quotes | 34pt | Regular |
| Subheader | 21pt | Medium |
| Body text | 21pt | Regular |

---

## 11. Letter-Spacing / Tracking (p.21, Section 3.0 Typography)

Reduced letter-spacing (tracking) is key to the Xweather typography.

| Element | Adobe Tracking | CSS letter-spacing |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Headlines | -30 | -0.03em |
| Ingress & quotes | -15 | -0.015em |
| Body copy | 0 | 0 |

Adjust proportionally in other platforms to maintain consistency.

---

## 12. Typography Don'ts (p.22, Section 3.0 Typography)

1. **Don't use uppercase titles** (exception: uppercase can be used for small overline headings)
2. **Don't use bolded ingress text or quotes**
3. **Don't use small body text** with thinner than the regular cut

## 14. Gradients (p.25, Section 3.5 Colors)

- The Xweather color palette can be applied as gradients
- **Don't default to basic linear gradients** — instead look for subtle gradients with an organic feel, like a shadow, sky, or blurry photo
- Examples show dark, moody, atmospheric gradient imagery mixing deep teals, blacks, and subtle light

---

## 15. Color Pairings and Hierarchy (p.26, Section 3.5 Colors)

- Color use is **limited, almost grayscale**, using distinct colors only for subtle details
- Structure information with: different shades of gray, font styles, layout — not color
- Color should only be applied to achieve the appropriate amount required for brand consistency
- **Prefer grayscale/muted colors**, photo or gradient backgrounds on large surfaces instead of solid green or orange

### Approved

- Grayscale cards with subtle color accents
- Photo/gradient backgrounds

### Not approved

- Large solid Misty Green or Dawn Orange backgrounds
- Heavy color usage for information hierarchy

---

## 16. Color Pairings — Cool/Warm Contrast (p.27, Section 3.5 Colors)

The cool/warm contrast (teal vs. orange tones) can be applied when presenting **two related products together**. Examples show side-by-side product cards: "Wind energy" + "Solar energy", "Road asset management" + "Winter maintenance", etc.

## 18. Layout Principles (p.30, Section 5 Layout)

- Follows the general Vaisala brand book layout principles
- Combines the **detailed nature of scientific papers** with a **clean, minimalistic modern UI** aesthetic
- Communicates industry-leading quality in both science and software development
- Examples show a grid of marketing materials: dark backgrounds, minimal text, modern typography, subtle imagery

---

## 19. Brand Images (p.32, Section 6 Imagery)

Brand imagery guidelines:

- Photos tied together by Xweather color palette and a **minimalistic, cinematic photography style**
- Weather is present through interesting lighting conditions: **dusk, dawn, or mist**
- Photos should be **carefully color graded** to appear like snapshots from the same film (range: dark to light, cool to warm)
- Range from **ultra-wide landscapes to abstract close-ups**
- Can include **partially blurry, underexposed, or out-of-focus images** for cinematic feel
- Prefer **modern/futuristic or timeless subjects** when portraying built environment, equipment, etc.

---

## 20. Product Images (p.33, Section 6 Imagery)

- Product images should reflect the **industry-leading and mission-critical** nature of Xweather products
- Apply Xweather color palette when possible
- Device mockups/in-use photos should reflect standards of top organizations
- **Prefer dark mode or dark base maps** in software when available
- When introducing features, show UI elements separately at **large enough scale** for clear communication
- Apply **animations** when possible to communicate the interactive nature of products

---

## 21. Vaisala Xweather Identity Overview (text page)

Key points:

- Vaisala Xweather has **three additional brand elements** reserved for its sole use (vs parent Vaisala):
  1. Vaisala Xweather logo
  2. Modified color scheme
  3. Additional behavior reflected in tone of voice: **Brave**
- Target group: B2B companies affected by weather
- Follows the master brand architecture contributing to Vaisala's category of **"instruments and intelligence for climate action"**
- **Vaisala Xweather is always referred to as a portfolio or platform** — never as a separate company
- Can be shortened to **"Xweather"** after first introduction as "Vaisala Xweather"

---

## 22. Design Language (text page)

Core design philosophy:

- Shaped by the mission: **protecting lives and assets, addressing climate change, supporting critical decision-making**
- **Calm layouts, minimal palette** — focus on what matters: the product, the thinking, the people
- A timeless foundation built to endure and adapt
- **Value attention to detail** over grabbing attention
- Every visual element should reflect **industry-leading quality**
- Goal: not to oversimplify what's complex, but to **respect the intelligence of the audience**
- Let design express **clarity and precision**
- Designed to **invite curiosity** and offer something meaningful for the right people to discover
